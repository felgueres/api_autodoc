Release Notes

The latest updates for Upstream AI

Written by Pablo Felgueres

Launching User-Trained Chatbots (Mar 8th 2023)

Upstream now lets you train AI chatbots with your content. Chatbots are a powerful way to build specialized AI assistants. This means you can combine multiple data sources and train an AI to answer questions specific to those sources.

This unlocks a new set of use cases for our customers. Here's a few examples:

  1. Chatbot to answer customer questions. Businesses of any size are able to train a chatbot on their company's website to add a new way for their customer's to know about their products and services.
  2. Chatbot for R&D. Teams are able to gather many research documents and papers into a chatbot to discuss new approaches and ideas.
  3. Chatbot for technical translation. Teams are able to train a chatbot on their technical documentation to help non-technical people understand their products.
  4. Chatbot for hobbyists. Hobbyists are able to train a chatbot on their hobby to help others learn about it.

I'm super excited to see what you build with Upstream AI.

Upgrading to ChatGPT (Feb 29th 2023)

Yesterday OpenAI released the model used in ChatGPT. This is the most advanced AI model out there and I've made the changes for Upstream to use it. Across the board it's a step change improvement, here's what it means for you:

  1. Faster response times.This model is 5-10X faster than the previous model which translates to noticeably lower waiting times to get answers.
  2. Better quality answers.This model is highly fine-tuned, producing much better quality answers.
  3. More relevant answers from your data.From user feedback, I've increased the response length and made changes to the PDF retrieval process to improve your results.

These are exciting times in AI -- with the world's best models publicly available, smaller teams like Upstream can focus on building deep, high value integrations for our customers.

Upstream with external data sources (Feb 28th 2023)

Upstream now lets you upload PDFs. This means you can get answers and back-and-forth dialogue using your PDF files as context. AI is a powerful to the extent that it helps you accomplish a task -- my goal with this integration is to get you factual answers, from your data sources, in no time.

It's super easy, this is how it works:

  1. Upload a PDF.
  2. Select the file by pressing the '/' key.
  3. Start a dialogue.

Under the hood, it's a two step process where your PDF and dialogue is converted into a vector space representation. Then Upstream does semantic search to the most relevant passages in the PDF and formulates an answer with GPT.
